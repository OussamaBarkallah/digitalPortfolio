Understanding the Relationship Between Older Adults and E-Health

Exploring Digital Health Through an Accessibility Lens

This thesis research explores how older adults (70+) interact with e-health services, focusing on usability, accessibility, and trust. The goal was to understand how digital health platforms can better support this growing population while reducing the digital divide.
Using Doctolib as a case study, I analyzed how seniors navigate digital healthcare tools and identified the main barriers they encounter—from limited digital literacy to interface complexity. The research aimed to strengthen autonomy, confidence, and inclusion in digital health experiences.

Research Questions

This study was guided by two central questions:

  • What causes the digital divide among older adults?
  • What challenges do they face when using e-health services?


Methodology

To answer these questions, I adopted a mixed UX research approach combining qualitative and quantitative methods:

  • Quantitative surveys with public organizations to analyze existing digital training programs for seniors
  • Ethnographic interviews with older adults to observe real-life interactions with Doctolib
  • Focus groups to encourage discussion, shared experiences, and collective reflection

Methodology process

Key Findings

All collected data was synthesized through a complete UX process, including:

  • Personas
  • User journeys
  • Experience maps
  • How Might We statements

These insights led to concrete design recommendations aimed at making e-health platforms more inclusive, intuitive, and empowering for elderly users.

Design Opportunities & Future Directions

The research highlights the need for adaptive interfaces that cater to declining cognitive or sensory abilities. Future directions include integrating simplified navigation modes, voice-assisted guidance, and clearer visual hierarchies to ensure that digital health tools act as an aid rather than a barrier.

Skills Gained

  • Designing and conducting interview protocols
  • Leading qualitative research and data synthesis
  • Applying UX methodologies to complex social and healthcare challenges
  • Proposing user-centered solutions adapted to older adults


Final Thoughts

This thesis strengthened my understanding of digital accessibility and empathy-driven design. It demonstrated how thoughtful UX research can reduce digital inequality and significantly improve access to healthcare for older generations.
