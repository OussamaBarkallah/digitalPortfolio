Interactive Kiosks in Pharmacies

Exploring Human-Centered Design in Healthcare Technology

This project examines the role of interactive kiosks in pharmacies and how users perceive automation in sensitive healthcare contexts. Through targeted user interviews, we explored whether self-service solutions truly meet patient needs or if they risk undermining trust and the essential human connection.

Research Approach

We conducted in-depth interviews with participants experienced in using interactive kiosks. While automation is widely accepted in retail and fast food, our research revealed significant reservations in healthcare—a domain patients consider deeply personal and rooted in trust.

Key Insights

  • Human Interaction is Irreplaceable: Participants highly value the reassurance, professional guidance, and empathy provided by pharmacists.
  • The Trust Gap: Replacing face-to-face consultation with full automation was perceived as impersonal and potentially unreliable for medication management.
  • Efficiency vs. Empathy: Speed is appreciated, but not at the cost of clinical safety and human storytelling.


Design Opportunities: The Hybrid Model

Rather than replacing human staff, the goal is to augment kiosks with AI-powered assistants. By integrating natural language processing, these systems can provide responsive communication while preserving the pharmacist's central role.

Final Thoughts

This project reinforced that in healthcare UX, efficiency alone is insufficient. Empathy and trust are the core components of any digital health solution. Technology must act as an assistive system that enhances—rather than replaces—the vital relationship between patients and healthcare professionals.
