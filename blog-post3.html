AI & Semiotics: The Text-Image Relationship

Exploring the Connection Between Text, Image, and AI

This project explores the complex interaction between language and visuals. By combining semiotic theory with AI-generation, we analyzed how meaning is created, translated, and transformed across different mediums. Our goal was to determine how AI can act as a bridge between narrative and visual representation.

Our Creative Process

We combined theoretical analysis with hands-on experimentation. Using Roland Barthes’ semiotic framework, we studied how text anchors or alters the interpretation of an image. We then reversed the process, using AI tools to translate complex textual narratives into visual storytelling.

Methodology & Exercises

Exercise 1 — From Text to Meaning:

Analyzing how linguistic descriptions guide the viewer's interpretation through denotation and connotation.

Exercise 2 — Generative Landscapes:

Generative Landscapes: Using Adobe Firefly, Bing Image Creator, and DALL·E to generate visuals inspired by the artistic style of Caspar David Friedrich.

AI & Semiotics: The Text-Image Relationship

Critical Evaluation: Documenting the technical strengths and limitations of each AI model regarding coherence and artistic intent.

What We Learned

This study deepened our understanding of AI as an extension of the analytical process. While AI can fluently translate text into images, human interpretation remains essential to evaluate context, nuance, and the final artistic meaning.

Final Thoughts

AI is a powerful tool for exploration, but not a substitute for human insight. True meaning-making and creative sensitivity still rely heavily on critical thinking and the designer's vision.
